Water Quality Targets In Transit

Dissolved Oxygen (DO)

Maintain 5-8 mg/L ideal range. Take action if DO approaches 3-5 mg/L.

pH

Keep around 7.0 to 7.5. Avoid extreme acidic or alkaline water.

Ammonia

Monitor continuously. Reduce risk when total ammonia builds above 5 mg/L; toxic level can occur around 2 ppm for sensitive conditions.

Temperature

Warm freshwater species generally perform better around 15-20 C during live transport.

Alkalinity

Use water with at least 90 mg CaCO3/L to improve pH stability.

Clean Water Source

Use water free from suspended solids, hydrogen sulphide, dissolved iron, and pollutants.

LFCS Operations: Practical Workflow

  1. Harvest fish in evening hours and pre-condition in hapa before loading.
  2. Fill LFCS tank with fresh pond water up to marked level.
  3. Connect power source and activate aerator/filter/shower/chiller as needed.
  4. Release fish into LFCS, cover tank, and monitor fish activity continuously.
  5. For long trips, perform partial water management and never fully drain during transit.
  6. After unloading, clean and disinfect filter pads and transport tank before next trip.

Do not feed fish during journey. Avoid abrupt noise, jolts, and overloading.

Pre-Transport & Arrival Checklist

  • Fast fish for at least 24 hours (species and temperature dependent) before movement.
  • Gradually acclimatize temperature: minimum 20 minutes per 5 C difference.
  • Keep fish in dark, calm conditions to reduce activity and oxygen demand.
  • Use insulated containers and reduce total travel time where possible.
  • At destination, equalize temperature first, then gradually exchange transport water.
